PLNLY · Visual Identity — Stage 07

Motion & voice.

The brand in time, and the brand in words. Motion is slow, certain, and rare — things arrive, they don't perform. Voice is plain language with one elevated word doing real work. Both are the personality made operational: capable, warm, restrained — it says it once and says it well.

01 — Motion principles
Arrive, don't perform.

Elements settle into place with a soft rise and fade. No bounce, no spin-for-show, no springy overshoot.

Slow and sure.

Gentle easing, generous duration. Calm reads as competence; speed reads as anxious.

Once is enough.

One considered motion per moment. If two things move for attention, cut one.

Durations
160ms · micro (hover)
320ms · standard (enter)
700ms · the reveal
Easing
cubic-bezier(.22,.61,.36,1)
— ease-out, no overshoot
Rule

Motion clarifies a change of state — never decorates a static one. Respect reduced-motion settings; the brand is just as calm holding still.

02 — The signature motion · the reveal
PLNLY
plainly
The name, then the breath.

The wordmark is present first. A beat later, the divider and plainly rise gently in beneath it — the pronunciation, revealed. It's the only place the brand performs, and it does so quietly: 700ms, one soft rise, once. Used on first load, intro animations, and openers — never on every page transition.

03 — The orbit, in motion
Almost imperceptibly slow.

When the orbit moves, it takes the better part of a minute to turn — closer to a clock's hour hand than a spinner. The satellites drift; the household at the center holds still. As a working state it speeds up just enough to read as "thinking." Either way it stays meditative, never busy.

Watch a moment — the colored points are slowly circling.

04 — Voice principles
Capable, not reassuring.

Assumes you're smart and busy, not worried. Names the real tool and the real task. Never "AI-powered solutions," never "don't worry."

Warm, with edges.

Treated as an equal, not hugged. One dry aside, rarely — never a brand-voice "joke" reaching to be liked.

One elevated word.

Plain prose with a single word doing real work — the same restraint as the italic in the type. Confidence by what's left out.

Specific over generic.

Name the tool, the task, the place. "Set up NotebookLM for your reading" beats "boost productivity."

05 — Says this · never that
✓ Says
"Here's the setup. You've got it from here."
"We'll set up your travel the way you actually pack."
"Built bespoke. Yours to run."
✕ Never
"Don't worry — it's so easy!"
"Unlock the power of AI to transform your life."
"Your AI-powered household solution."
