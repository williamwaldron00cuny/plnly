PLNLY · Visual Identity — Stage 06

Icon & imagery.

The brand's quietest opinions live here. Icons are geometric and abstract — built from the dot, the ring, and the line, not from literal pictures. Imagery is warm, real, and a little held-back. And one recurring motif — a quiet orbit — carries the cosmic, "if you know, you know" feeling without a single circuit board or robot in sight.

01 — Icon principles
One stroke, one geometry.

Built on a 24px grid with a consistent 1.75px stroke, round caps and joins. Circles and straight lines do most of the work. Coral appears in at most one element of an icon, and only when it carries meaning — a focal point, an active state. The result reads as a considered system, not a downloaded set.

02 — The marks · abstract before literal

PLNLY's expressive icons are built from primitives, so meaning is suggested, not spelled out — fitting for a brand with a little mystery. (For dense functional UI, a thin-line library set to this stroke and color fills the gaps.)

03 — The quiet orbit · the cosmic nod
A system, in orbit around you.

Concentric hairline rings with a coral center — the household at the middle, the tools quietly circling. It's the "cosmic, but not a laboratory" feeling you described: ethereal, intriguing, drawn from pure geometry. Use it large and faint as a backdrop, or small as a section marker.

This replaces every AI cliché — no circuits, no neural lines, no robots. Just orbit.

04 — Imagery direction

Warm, real, unhurried. Soft natural light, lived-in interiors, hands and objects over faces, generous negative space. Park Hyatt calm meets Aesop restraint — never stock-photo "happy family at laptop." Placeholders below name the shot; drop real photography in later.

◳  morning light, quiet kitchen
◳  hands at a laptop
◳  keys, notebook
or: an orbit field, no photo at all
DO  — soft light · real rooms · objects & hands · space to breathe
AVOID — robots · circuits · neon · stock "tech" · cold blue gradients
05 — Texture · flat digital, textured print
Screen — flat

Clean, matte color. No gradients, no noise. Light comes from layout and space, not effects.

Print — textured

Uncoated, tactile stock with a faint tooth. Cards and the welcome packet get warmth you can feel — Aesop, not glossy.
