PLNLY · Visual Identity — Stage 04

Typography.

Four voices, each with one job. Outfit speaks plainly and does the headlines. Newsreader, in italic, is the one elevated word — warmth that reads as taste, not softness. Hanken Grotesk is the quiet workhorse for everything you actually read. The mono labels and times the brand, like a stamp. Restraint is the rule: most pages use two of these, never all four at once.

01 — The four voices
Outfit
Display & headline · the wordmark

Geometric with humanist warmth. Plain, modern, certain. Light–Medium weights only; never bold.

Newsreader
The elevated word · the reveal

Used in italic, sparingly — one phrase, one pull-quote, the plainly reveal. The brand's single human gesture.

Hanken
Body & interface · the workhorse

Calm, legible grotesque for everything read at length. Recedes so the headline and the reveal can speak.

Mono
Labels & data · the stamp

Eyebrows, captions, coordinates, fine print. Tracked wide and uppercase — it dates and locates the brand.

03 — The elevated word
Built bespoke. Yours to run.
A beautiful plainness.
One word, doing real work.

Newsreader italic appears once in a composition — the turn in a headline, a single emphasized word, the plainly reveal. It carries the warmth so the rest can stay plain. Two italic moments on one screen is one too many.

Rule: never set a whole sentence in the italic, and never use it for UI labels — it's a flourish, not a system font.

04 — Body & interface · Hanken Grotesk

PLNLY sets the tools of modern life up around how a household actually lives, teaches the household to run them, and hands over the keys. The work is configured around scheduling, groceries, travel, and the ordinary operations of a home — then it's yours.

Set at 16px for body and 1.6 line-height for comfortable reading. Measure stays between 45 and 75 characters — long enough to breathe, short enough to track.
