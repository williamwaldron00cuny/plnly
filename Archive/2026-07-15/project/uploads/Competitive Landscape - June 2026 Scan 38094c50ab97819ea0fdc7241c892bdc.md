# Competitive Landscape — June 2026 Scan

*Filed ahead of visual identity work, to test whether "AI for Regular Folks" is open territory and whether the no-jargon framing actually differentiates PLNLY.*

## Bottom line

The space is not empty, but nobody is doing the specific combination PLNLY is proposing — in-person, Brooklyn-based, ongoing-relationship AI help, wrapped in a design-forward personal brand. The risk isn't a local competitor beating PLNLY to market; it's that the "no jargon, AI made simple" *language* is already crowded nationally, so that phrase alone won't be the differentiator. What will differentiate PLNLY is the in-home relationship, the Brooklyn locality, and the design quality — not the jargon-free claim by itself.

No evidence surfaced of a Brooklyn or NYC-specific competitor about to claim this exact lane. The real time pressure is locking the name, domain, and handles — not racing a rival.

## What already exists

**Free / institutional** — NYPL's TechConnect runs 100+ free tech classes across the boroughs, multilingual, covering everything from basic computer use to iPhone series courses. Senior Planet (run by OATS, founded in NYC in 2004) offers free online classes for 60+ in English, Spanish, and Chinese. NYC Older Adult Centers bundle computer classes in with meals and fitness programs. This is the "why would I pay" baseline PLNLY has to answer.

**Paid in-person tech tutors (not AI-specific)** — Senior Tech Tutors operates in NYC and Philly: one-on-one, in-home, hourly, with a discount for buying 3 hours at a time. Their pitch is almost word-for-word the gap PLNLY is filling ("there when your family has given up and the Apple Store geniuses have kicked you out") but the focus is general device literacy, not AI tools specifically. Your Tech Tutor (NY-based) has started folding ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini into general tech support — "use AI to plan a vacation or restore old photos" — but it's an add-on to general tutoring, not the headline.

**National "AI made simple" coaches** — Tech Tutor USA runs the closest message-match to PLNLY's current framing: "No technical jargon. No pressure... I help everyday people — especially beginners, seniors, and Executives — understand AI and ChatGPT." Their model is workshops for Chambers of Commerce, Rotary Clubs, HOAs, and senior groups — national, remote/event-based, not an ongoing in-home relationship. This confirms the *language* of "no jargon AI help" is already out there; PLNLY needs a different hook than the phrase itself.

**Kids/teens AI + coding** — [codingtutor.nyc](http://codingtutor.nyc) offers in-person and Zoom lessons in Claude Code, AI-assisted app building, JavaScript, React, and Scratch for kids through adults. Different lane (coding skills vs. AI literacy/safe use) but worth knowing it exists in the same NYC tutoring ecosystem.

**Teen AI safety — a live moment, not a niche** — In December 2025, OpenAI published new teen-specific model rules and two AI-literacy guides for parents and teens, developed with the American Psychological Association and ConnectSafely. In March 2026, Google and [Character.AI](http://Character.AI) settled multiple wrongful-death lawsuits brought by families whose kids died after using the platform — the most serious child-safety failure documented in consumer AI so far. NPR's Life Kit ran a segment in fall 2025 on how parents should talk to teens about AI. One stat worth keeping: roughly 70% of kids use AI, but only about 37% of parents know it.

## Pricing benchmarks

General private tutoring in NYC runs $25–80/hr depending on subject and experience. ChatGPT-specific tutoring on marketplaces like Superprof averages around $27/hr. Senior Tech Tutors uses an hourly rate with a bulk discount at 3 hours. On the retainer side (relevant to the "ongoing management" tier), virtual-assistant-style monthly retainers for individuals/families run roughly $100–250/month for a lighter touch, climbing to $1,800–2,200+/month for a dedicated, full-scope arrangement — useful as a rough band when pricing PLNLY's ongoing tier, though PLNLY's scope (AI tool management, not general admin) isn't a clean match to either end.

## Concerns to address

**1. The "it's free at the library" objection.** NYPL and Senior Planet are free, good, and well-known. PLNLY's pitch needs to name the difference directly: this is in *your* home, on *your* devices and accounts, at *your* pace, with someone who comes back — not a class with strangers on a library schedule. This should show up explicitly in the site copy, not be left implied.

**2. "No jargon AI help" isn't a novel claim.** Tech Tutor USA is already saying almost exactly this, nationally. The jargon-free promise should stay as the *tone*, but the headline differentiator needs to be the in-person Brooklyn relationship and the design quality of the brand itself — things a remote, workshop-based national competitor structurally can't match.

**3. Teen AI-safety scope needs a hard boundary.** This is the one that matters most. The teen/AI-literacy space is currently tangled up with real lawsuits, mental health crises, and wrongful-death settlements ([Character.AI](http://Character.AI), March 2026). Given that Billy is heading toward an MSW but isn't yet credentialed, any teen-facing offering needs to be scoped, in writing, as practical AI literacy and safe-tool setup — not counseling, not crisis support, not mental health guidance. This isn't just a marketing nuance; it's a liability boundary that should be reflected in the service descriptions, intake forms, and any waiver language before a single teen client is taken on. Worth a short conversation with a lawyer or at minimum a liability-insurance check before this sub-offering goes live, even in soft-launch form.

**4. Timing.** No Brooklyn-specific competitor was found occupying this exact lane. The macro environment (AI anxiety, teen-safety headlines, ChatGPT going mainstream for non-tech people) supports launching in 2026 — but there's no evidence of a local rival about to take the spot. The urgency is in securing PLNLY's own name and handles, not in beating someone else to the idea.

## Suggested mission tweak

No wholesale change needed. Two adjustments worth carrying into the brand voice and service copy:

- Reframe the headline promise away from "we'll explain AI without jargon" (a claim others are already making) toward the relationship and locality: an actual person, in Brooklyn, who comes back. The jargon-free tone stays — it's just not the *headline* anymore.
- Add an explicit scope line to the teen/AI-literacy offering distinguishing it from mental health support, both for the website copy and for Billy's own protection once that service line is active.
