# Core AI Service — Offer & Go-to-Market

> The first paid offering under the brand, and the fastest path to real income. Personal AI help for real life — not for your business, for your household.
> 

## Positioning

*(Updated after two rounds of competitive research — see [Round 1](../Competitive%20Landscape%20%E2%80%94%20June%202026%20Scan%2038094c50ab97819ea0fdc7241c892bdc.md) and [Round 2](../Competitive%20Landscape%20%E2%80%94%20Round%202%20(Affluent%20Househol%2038094c50ab9781ae83d1e91d6dbadd69.md).)*

A Brooklyn-based service for busy households who could probably figure this out themselves — if they had a free weekend and the patience for a stack of YouTube tutorials. PLNLY is the person who shows up, configures the tools around how the household actually runs, and teaches everyone to use them — so the family ends up *more* capable, not more dependent. That's the deliberate opposite of a delegation service (a human or AI assistant that just keeps doing the task for you): PLNLY hands over the keys.

## Who it's for (two segments)

1. **Busy, time-constrained households** — the lead segment. Affluent families who want AI to take the edge off life admin: scheduling, groceries, bills, appointments, travel planning — while staying in control of their own systems rather than handing them to an assistant. The offer is framed as a capability, not a closed menu: a household should see its own need in it, travel included, even where it isn't spelled out.
2. **Parents of college-bound teens** — want their kid using tools like NotebookLM well for research, citations, and applications: tool literacy, not subject tutoring, squarely inside academic-integrity lines. *(Sub-offering naming still open — see Launch Plan, Week 2.)*

## Packages (productized — prices are starting points to test against NYC in-home rates)

- **Kickstart — one session (~90 min), flat fee (~$150–250).** Set up the right AI tools on their devices for their goals; leave a one-page cheat sheet. The easy yes / front door.
- **Household AI Setup — 2–3 sessions, flat fee (~$500–900).** Full setup + practical workflows/agents (calendars, meal plans, homework guardrails, document help) + a custom "household playbook."
- **AI Concierge — monthly retainer (~$99–199/mo).** The recurring revenue: ongoing tweaks, add new skills as they need them, a monthly check-in, priority Q&A. As they get better, you add functions.
- **Add-ons:** Teen AI-Literacy session (parent present); small-group workshop priced per head — great at libraries, parent groups, and LGBTQ+ orgs (mission + lead gen).

Offer a **founding-client rate** to your first 5 in exchange for testimonials and referrals. Price for *your* time, not theirs.

## Trust & safety (this is differentiation, not just risk)

- Clear written scope + a simple service agreement.
- For minors: parent present, always.
- Look into a basic background check and liability/insurance for in-home work (worth checking; not legal advice).
- A plain data-privacy promise: you don't keep their personal info. Say it out loud — it's a selling point.

## Go-to-market (no budget, first 10 clients)

- **Warm network first.** Your first 5 clients come from people who already trust you — friends, neighbors, your building, the dog park, your communities. Start there.
- **Local posts.** Brooklyn neighborhood groups (Nextdoor, local FB groups, parent lists), plus library and community-center bulletin boards.
- **Partner with community orgs.** LGBTQ+ centers, libraries, and parent/community groups: offer a free intro workshop → paid clients. Mission and funnel at once.
- **Build-in-public content.** Short "AI for real life" tip clips — same content engine as ChartQuest. Doubles as brand marketing.
- **Simple plumbing.** One-page site + Calendly booking + Stripe/Square for payment. That's the whole tech stack to start.
