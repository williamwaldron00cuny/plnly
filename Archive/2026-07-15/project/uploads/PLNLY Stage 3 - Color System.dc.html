PLNLY · Visual Identity — Stage 03

The color system.

Greige leads. Ink anchors. A small family of layered hues — blue, eucalyptus, and the mint you wanted — sit quietly underneath, and one coral accent does the loud work, rarely. Each color has a role and a ratio; that's what keeps the brand calm instead of busy. The hues also do double duty: each is a candidate signature tone for a future sub-brand.

03 — The layered hues · secondary, ~8%

Soft, desaturated, never loud. Used as small tints, blocks, and illustration fills — a quiet wash of color, never a field that competes with coral. The two darks (Harbor, Eucalyptus) can also stand in for ink on a richer surface. Mint is the freshest of the set — a strong candidate for the first named sub-brand's signature tone.

04 — The accent · coral, ~2%
Coral
#E25E3A · CORAL DEEP #B8492A
One accent. Used rarely.

Coral lives on the plainly reveal, the dot, one link or one button per view, and the rare loud moment. If a screen has more than one coral thing fighting for attention, remove one. Coral Deep is the text-safe version for coral words on a light ground and for hover states.

05 — Functional · UI only, muted to match

Status colors for interfaces — forms, confirmations, alerts. Drawn from the same desaturated family so a success message never looks like it wandered in from another brand. Error sits deliberately deeper and redder than brand coral, so the two never get confused. Caution is earthy amber, not lemon — kept small.
