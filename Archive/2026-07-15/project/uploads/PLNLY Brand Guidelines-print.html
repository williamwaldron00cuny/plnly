01 — Strategy & positioning
An ownership brand, not a concierge. We set the tools up, teach you to run them, and hand over the keys.
Promise

Modern life, minus the jargon. Capability you keep — warm, considered, above the fray.

Tagline

Built bespoke. Yours to run. The ownership idea in four words.

Audiences

Busy households who want to own the capability; parents readying college-bound teens for responsible AI literacy.

Warm, not eager A little mysterious Competent, never patronizing Quietly high-end

05 — Spacing & grid

Eight-point rhythm. Twelve columns.

Space is the brand's loudest material. When in doubt, add room — not ornament.

Spacing scale · multiples of 8

4–32 inside components · 48–64 between blocks · 96–128 between sections.

12 columns · radius · elevation
2 · 4 · 12 · pill
06 — Icon, imagery & motif

Abstract before literal. A system in orbit.

Icons built from the dot, the ring, the line — 1.75px stroke, round caps. The orbit is the cosmic nod that replaces every AI cliché.

DO — soft light · real rooms · objects & hands · space to breathe · the orbit, large & faint
AVOID — robots · circuits · neon · stock “tech” · cold blue gradients
07 — Motion & voice

Arrive, don't perform. Say it once.

Motion is slow and rare: 160 / 320 / 700ms, gentle ease-out, no overshoot. Voice is plain language, capable not reassuring, with one elevated word.

✓ Sounds like
“Here's the setup. You've got it from here.”
“Built bespoke. Yours to run.”
✕ Never
“Don't worry — it's so easy!”
“Unlock the power of AI to transform your life.”
08 — The family & PLAINLY

The two letters we left out.

PLNLY is “plainly” with the A and the I removed — modern life, minus the jargon, and quietly minus the “AI.” The first sub-brand, the AI-literacy program, is the one place we put them back. Future named lines inherit the same DNA with their own secondary hue.

The umbrella endorses from above; each property keeps its own front door. Shared: the geometric wordmark, the coral plainly, the dot, the orbit. The naming convention for future lines is deferred — nothing is invented ahead of the brief.
