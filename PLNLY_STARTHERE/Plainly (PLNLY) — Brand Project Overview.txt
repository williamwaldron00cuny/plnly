Plainly (PLNLY) — Brand Project Overview

A portable brief for this project. Move it into its own Claude space and Notion hub so the brand work runs separately from ChartQuest.

The idea
An umbrella personal brand that makes the intimidating modern stuff approachable for regular people — not businesses, not finance pros, not tech insiders. Warm, plain-spoken, design-forward, jargon-free. Billy is the visible face. The brand sits one notch above "AI" so future spokes (ChartQuest, curations, a blog) fit without a rebrand.

Promise (working): "Modern life, minus the jargon."

First offering (= the brand, for now)
AI for Regular Folks — the near-term earner. Personal AI help for real life:
- In-home / remote setup of the right AI tools for someone's goals.
- Teaching people (and families) to use them well.
- Ongoing management (monthly retainer) that adds skills as they're ready.
- Teens / pre-college: a leg up and parent peace-of-mind that they're using AI appropriately.
Audience: everyday people and families in Brooklyn/NYC first, then online.

Naming — decision in progress
Drawn to "Plainly." The snag: bare "Plainly" is crowded in software/AI (plainlyvideos.com, Plainly Legal, Plainly Software…) and the good domains are taken — a plain dictionary word used by many is weak and hard to own.

Direction: keep "Plainly" as the spoken name, use a distinctive stylized wordmark/handle "PLNLY" (add vowels back for readable/AI contexts). 
- Available & promising: plnly.co / .io / .ai, plainlynyc.com, plainlybrooklyn.com. No existing "PLNLY" brand found.
- Before committing money: run a free USPTO trademark search, consider a trademark attorney, and confirm plnly.com status. (Not legal advice.)

How business naming works (the layers)
- Domain / social handles — first-come, technical; no legal rights by themselves.
- Business entity name (NY Dept. of State LLC/DBA) — lets you operate under the name in NY; not brand protection.
- Trademark — the real protection. Federal (USPTO), nationwide, class-based (same name can coexist in unrelated industries). Distinctive/coined marks (like PLNLY) are far easier to own than common words.
